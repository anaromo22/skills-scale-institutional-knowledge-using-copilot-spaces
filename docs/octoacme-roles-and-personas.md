# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## Release Manager

### Role Summary
Release Managers coordinate and oversee the release process, ensuring all pre-release requirements are met, stakeholders are informed, and deployments proceed safely with minimal risk.

### Responsibilities
- Schedule and communicate release timelines to all stakeholders
- Verify that all acceptance criteria, tests, and security scans are complete before deployment
- Coordinate with DevOps on deployment scheduling and rollback plans
- Manage release notes and communicate changes to support and customers
- Triage and escalate deployment issues in real-time
- Coordinate post-deployment verification and incident response if needed

### Goals
- Achieve reliable, predictable releases with minimal downtime
- Reduce release-related incidents and rollbacks
- Keep all stakeholders informed and aligned on release status

### Typical Communication
- Pre-release coordination with engineering and DevOps
- Release notes and stakeholder announcements
- Incident communication and status updates during/after deployment
- Post-release retrospectives and lessons learned

---

## QA Lead

### Role Summary
QA Leads define quality standards, test strategies, and acceptance criteria. They own the quality gates and work with Developers and Product Managers to ensure features meet functional and non-functional requirements before release.

### Responsibilities
- Define and document the test strategy for each project phase
- Establish quality gates (when manual vs. automated testing is required)
- Create and review acceptance criteria with Product Managers and Developers
- Lead manual testing efforts and triage defects
- Collaborate on automated test coverage and integration test design
- Sign off on feature readiness before release

### Goals
- Maintain high product quality and customer satisfaction
- Catch defects early and reduce production issues
- Balance testing rigor with delivery velocity

### Typical Communication
- Feature acceptance criteria reviews with Product Managers
- Test plan and strategy documentation
- Daily standup updates on quality metrics and blockers
- Defect triage and escalation to engineering leads

---

## DevOps Engineer

### Role Summary
DevOps Engineers design, build, and maintain CI/CD pipelines, deployment infrastructure, and operational tooling. They enable fast, safe, and automated delivery of software.

### Responsibilities
- Build and maintain CI/CD pipelines for automated testing and deployment
- Manage deployment environments (staging, production) and infrastructure
- Ensure monitoring, logging, and alerting are configured for observability
- Support incident response and post-incident remediation
- Collaborate with Developers on deployability and testability
- Document runbooks and deployment procedures

### Goals
- Enable fast, reliable, and repeatable deployments
- Minimize manual deployment steps and human error
- Maintain system uptime and performance

### Typical Communication
- Deployment coordination with Release Manager and engineering
- Infrastructure and pipeline updates
- Incident response and post-mortem notes
- Documentation in runbooks and team wikis

---

## Technical Writer

### Role Summary
Technical Writers ensure that all project and product documentation is accurate, current, comprehensive, and accessible. They work with all team members to capture processes, features, and changes.

### Responsibilities
- Document project processes, workflows, and decision rationale
- Create and maintain user-facing documentation and help content
- Capture release notes and change logs
- Review and update process docs based on lessons learned and feedback
- Ensure clarity and consistency across all documentation

### Goals
- Reduce onboarding time for new team members
- Minimize support requests through clear, accessible documentation
- Preserve institutional knowledge and project history

### Typical Communication
- Process documentation and retrospective capture
- Release notes and change log updates
- Documentation reviews and feedback from stakeholders
- Process improvement suggestions based on documentation gaps

---

## UX Designer

### Role Summary
UX Designers champion user-centric design throughout the project lifecycle. They ensure that features are intuitive, accessible, and deliver a positive user experience.

### Responsibilities
- Collaborate with Product Managers to understand user needs and pain points
- Create design specifications, wireframes, and prototypes
- Conduct usability testing and gather user feedback
- Review features for accessibility standards (WCAG compliance)
- Coordinate design handoffs to Developers
- Advocate for usability and accessibility in prioritization discussions

### Goals
- Deliver features that users find intuitive and valuable
- Ensure accessibility and inclusive design practices
- Reduce user friction and support burden through good UX

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews and feedback with Product Managers and Developers
- User research findings and design rationale
- Accessibility and usability guidance during planning and execution
- Design system and component documentation
